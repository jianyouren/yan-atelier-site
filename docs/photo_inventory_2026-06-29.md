## What AutoEdit should NOT attempt

These categories produce uncanny tells regardless of model quality. Do not waste runs:

1. **Real human hand on real product** — fingerprint, nail growth, skin texture, jewelry-on-skin shadow, denim/silk drape interaction
2. **B&W documentary unposed** — relies on environmental clutter authenticity (tool placement randomness, sweat marks, work-in-progress chaos). AI 'documentary B&W' = stock photo aesthetic.
3. **Maker portrait variations of named individual** — identity claim risk. We have ONE real Hu Yan photo; AI cannot multiply it without identity fabrication.
4. **Press / award / event documentation** — these need real provenance.

## What AutoEdit handles well (continue using)

1. PDP product on neutral / dark / textured background
2. Hero atmospheric (no humans, no workshops with people)
3. Series banner with product alone
4. Detail shots emphasizing color/material/symmetry that don't require uncanny realism
5. Repair / variant fills for an existing real product photo
