## Synthesis A — Top 5 patterns YÀN **should learn**

1. **Heritage as footnote, not hero (Alighieri).** Dante is the brand's whole reason for existing, but it lives mid-page behind "Read the story", and the hero is just product. → YÀN: plique-à-jour history + Yunnan should sit as a *Craft* page link and a one-line meta, NOT as the first sentence the visitor reads. (alighieri.com — Dante text appears below the carousel as a tagline strip, full essay one click deep.)
2. **Designer-as-auteur navigation order (Anna Hu, Sophie Bille Brahe).** Anna Hu nav opens with `Artist / Timeline`; Sophie's penultimate item is `Meet Sophie Bille Brahe`. Both put the maker name in the nav itself, not behind "About". → YÀN: surface "Hu Yan" as a top-level nav item ("Maker" or her name), not buried under About.
3. **Photography = warm-light still-life + workbench environment, not on-model fashion (WWAKE, SBB, Pippa).** All three lead with object-on-neutral or object-in-process. Zero hero uses a styled fashion shot. → YÀN: hero should be a single piece in warm window light or a hands-at-bench frame. (wwake.com — "Continuum" series literally uses workbench shots as content.)
4. **Origin lives in product names and collection labels, never in chrome (Pippa Small, Alighieri, Anna Hu).** Pippa's collections are named "Jaipur / Colombia / Afghanistan"; Anna Hu's pieces are "Dunhuang Pipa"; Alighieri uses founder narrative. Country-of-origin **never** shows up as a flag, ornament, vertical script, or border. → YÀN: kill all visual "this is from China" signals from chrome (any remaining vertical CN, decorative dingbats, ornamental sections). Push origin into piece names and a Craft page.
5. **Sub-brand editorial chapters as merchandising (Alighieri "Chapters" + "CASA", Pippa "Stories" split Read/Watch/Listen, WWAKE "Continuum Journal").** All three use named editorial constructs to give product grouping a literary feel without inflating chrome. → YÀN: an editorial container (e.g. "Notes from the Workshop" or "Plate Series") to host Craft + Maker + selected SKUs without needing a heavy About section.

## Synthesis B — Top 3 anti-patterns YÀN **must NOT copy**

1. **The Mejuri/Aurate cream-soft-natural-light DTC template.** Sole brand in this set that arguably gets close is WWAKE, but WWAKE rescues itself via workbench shots + editorial Journal. If YÀN goes cream + soft window light + sans-serif + 18kt-gold + "ethically made in [city]" without YÀN's craft difference at hero, it disappears into a 20-brand visual paste. (Aurate blocked direct fetch but is the explicit reference category in the brief.) → Avoid: full-bleed cream background + soft-glow on-model lifestyle hero + flat sans + generic "fine jewelry made for everyday" tagline.
2. **Heavy ethical-supply-chain text up front (Pippa Small at scale, but only she earns it).** Pippa has 7 country menu items, "Our Artisans" sub-nav, and 15+ years of documented Turquoise Mountain / fair-mined provenance. A small brand attempting the same volume of ethical narrative looks performative and academic, not lived. → YÀN at $180–$700 should NOT lead with a long "ethical" paragraph; one line about Yunnan + 800 °C is enough.
3. **Decorative ornaments / dingbats / vertical type as "elegance signal".** Zero of the verified 5 sites use vertical script, dingbat dividers, ornamental borders, or "founder country" visual codes. Even Anna Hu, who is **explicitly Chinese-heritage haute joaillerie**, runs neutral-luxury chrome. The closest thing in the set is Alighieri's trailing underscore (`_`) and a tiny gold-puddle icon — that is the entire ornament budget. → YÀN: strip every remaining vertical CN ornament, paper-grain noise overlay used as "atelier feel", and Plate-No./Edition/Volume monograph chrome (the 2026-06-03 evidence-led recalibration already partially did this — finish the job).

## Synthesis C — Where does plique-à-jour + Yunnan substance go in this aesthetic vocabulary?

The verified precedent says: **substance lives in three slots, none of which is the hero.**

- **Slot 1 — Piece/Series names.** Like Anna Hu's "Dunhuang Pipa" and Pippa's "Jaipur": the cultural specificity is encoded in product nomenclature. YÀN's plique-à-jour and Yunnan should surface as named series ("Yunnan Cloisonné Plate"-style naming) or in the SKU title block (e.g. "Iris Lattice / plique-à-jour, 800 °C / No. 0005"). The technique becomes the product name, not a banner.
- **Slot 2 — A "Craft" page one click from nav.** Alighieri's Dante story sits behind "Read the story"; WWAKE's process sits inside "Continuum Journal"; Pippa's artisan story sits inside "About → Our Artisans". YÀN already has a Craft page; the rule is: it has to be ONE link from nav, and the homepage hero gets at most a one-line meta pointing to it.
- **Slot 3 — A "Maker" item in primary nav, named.** Anna Hu's `Artist` and SBB's `Meet Sophie Bille Brahe` both promote the founder to top-level. YÀN should have a primary-nav entry for Hu Yan / "Maker" — but the page itself can stay restrained and avoid Mother-Son personal-narrative voice (matches existing atelier-voice rule).

The hero itself should be a single piece, warm window light, neutral cream-or-darker ground (Anna Hu uses dark/white contrast; SBB uses near-white; Alighieri uses off-white — all three work, all three avoid Mejuri's identical-cream trap by photo register, not by site chrome), a 3-second readable buyer-side line, and a single "Shop" or "See piece" CTA. Plique-à-jour the word appears in the meta below the hero, in the piece name, and on the Craft page — not in the H1.

**Direct implication for current YÀN state:** the 2026-06-03 evidence-led recalibration (cut Plate No. monograph chrome, demote vertical CN ornament, frame Yunnan as workshop location) is **directionally correct** vs. all 5 verified precedents. The 2026-06-18 hero copy lock ("Wear it. People will ask where you found it.") is **buyer-side first-person**, which matches the precedent that NONE of the 5 leads with "we make X" or "X heritage" copy at hero. Continue this direction; do not let any "luxury Chinese heritage" framework re-introduce ornament/dingbat/vertical type onto chrome.
