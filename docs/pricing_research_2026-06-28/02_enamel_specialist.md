# Enamel Specialist + Plique-à-jour Benchmark

**Date:** 2026-06-28
**Scope:** Real catalog + auction price data for enamel-specialist jewelry, with emphasis on plique-à-jour and cloisonné. Compared to YÀN Atelier's current public USD range $179–$1,479 (CNY ¥680–¥5,180).

---

## Methodology + sources

- **Direct catalog WebFetch** on brand sites where retail prices are public (Loquet London, Smith & Bevill, dsfantiquejewelry, etc.).
- **Aggregator + dealer data** where the brand itself is "Call For Price" (Masriera at Lustre Gallery + Smith & Bevill; 1stDibs aggregate stats).
- **Auction records** (Bonhams, Rago, Sotheby's, LiveAuctioneers Masriera price guide).
- **Web search corroboration** for ranges (Cathy Waterman, Sevan Bıçakçı, Alice Cicolini).
- **Honest gaps:** Anna e Alex catalog page is behind a JS shop wrapper that returned no parseable price data on three attempts — Anna e Alex's resin-framed enamel sits roughly in the €150–€500 costume-jewelry register based on YOOX-style stockists, but I could not verify a clean number. **Cathy Waterman** website lists no prices; only resale + stockist references were verifiable. Recommend user spot-check Anna e Alex and Cathy Waterman directly before quoting.

**Currency:** Prices kept in original currency where verified. Conversions use approx Jun 2026 rates: €1 ≈ $1.10; £1 ≈ $1.28; ¥1 (CNY) ≈ $0.14.

## Enamel premium over non-enamel (where verifiable)

Within brands that do BOTH enamel and plain pieces:

- **Loquet London:** 1 enamel charm at £300 vs gold/gem charms £950–£1,100. Enamel here is **cheaper** than gem versions — because Loquet enamel is the entry decoration, not the headline. This is the inverse of what YÀN should expect.
- **Alice Cicolini:** her entire signature **is** enamel (Memphis lacquer + Jaipuri meenakari), so there is no in-house "plain version" to compare. Enamel IS the premium product. Entry $1,310, mid $1,400–$2,575.
- **Cathy Waterman:** enamel earrings (Turquoise Enamel & Diamond Flower) at $3,200 sit mid-range relative to her non-enamel platinum/diamond bands $2,500–$3,740 — i.e. roughly **parity, not a premium**, because at the 22K + diamond level the gold/stone cost dominates over enamel labour.
- **Masriera:** virtually every signature SKU is plique-à-jour, so there is no apples-to-apples plain equivalent. Their cross pendants at $5,690 reg. include both the gold base and the plique work — there is no $X-cheaper plain version to compare.

**Conclusion:** the "enamel premium" is not a clean number across the category. What IS clean: **brands whose identity = enamel** (Masriera, Cicolini, YÀN) price enamel as the signature product; brands that do enamel as a side technique (Loquet, Cathy Waterman) often price enamel BELOW gem-set equivalents because gem/metal cost dominates.

## Plique-à-jour vs cloisonné premium

This is the highest-leverage comparison for YÀN.

- **Plique-à-jour Masriera floor:** ~$3,983 (cross pendants on sale; $5,690 reg.) → ~$5,341 (mid earrings on sale; $7,630 reg.) → up to $59,125 (1stDibs ceiling).
- **Cloisonné contemporary maker pricing is much harder to isolate** because most contemporary "cloisonné" work in the West is hobbyist/Etsy register ($50–$500) or Chinese mainland export ($30–$300 wholesale). Fine-jewelry cloisonné as a Western signature is rare — Anna E Alex's "Arte Miniature" line approximates this but at resin-frame costume prices (~€150–€500 est.).
- **Auction premium:** plique-à-jour Lalique/Vever/Mellerio carry a clear premium over equivalent-period cloisonné. Lalique plique pendants clear $50K+ at Bonhams; Vever plique butterflies clear $25K+ at Rago. Comparable Art Nouveau cloisonné pieces typically clear $1K–$10K.
- **Rough heuristic from the data:** fine plique-à-jour commands **roughly 3–5× the price of comparable-tier cloisonné** in both contemporary catalog and secondary market. The premium comes from (a) very few workshops worldwide can fire it reliably, (b) higher failure rate per piece, (c) the translucent "stained glass" aesthetic reads as more precious.

## Where YÀN's $179–$1,479 sits in the enamel specialist map

**Entry ($179 / ¥680):**
- Sits BELOW the antique-resale floor for plique-à-jour ($350 1stDibs floor, $950 dsfantiquejewelry entry brooch).
- Sits BELOW Alice Cicolini's entry ($1,310).
- Sits AT Loquet's enamel charm price level (£300 ≈ $384) — but Loquet enamel is a gift-charm category, not signature plique.
- Roughly aligned with Anna e Alex resin-frame miniatures (estimated €150–€280 range).
- **If $179 is for a plique-à-jour piece, it is dramatically underpriced for the technique.** If it is for a non-plique enamel item (smaller cloisonné stud, simple painted enamel earring), it is in line with the lower-end Italian / fashion-enamel register.

**Top ($1,479 / ¥5,180):**
- Sits at roughly **27–30% of Masriera's plique-à-jour entry retail** ($5,690 cross pendant reg.; $3,983 on sale).
- Sits at roughly **60% of Alice Cicolini's mid Memphis range** ($2,575).
- Sits at roughly **46% of a verified Cathy Waterman enamel earring** ($3,200).
- Sits BELOW Mellerio antique plique brooch resale ($9,850), Shreve & Co. ($14,500), Vever auction ($27,720).
- **For a plique-à-jour piece comparable to Masriera's revival work, $1,479 is well under half of the closest direct peer's entry price.**

## Honest take — is YÀN underpriced / right / overpriced for the enamel specialist niche?

**On the plique-à-jour technique specifically: YÀN is underpriced — measurably, against the only living direct peer (Masriera) and against the auction record.**

The data:
- Masriera, the only continuously-operating Western workshop firing plique-à-jour at retail, prices entry plique-à-jour cross pendants at **$5,690 reg. ($3,983 on sale)**. YÀN's top SKU is $1,479 — roughly **26% of Masriera's entry-retail plique benchmark, 37% of their on-sale floor.**
- 1stDibs aggregate avg for plique-à-jour brooches alone is $4,748. YÀN's whole catalog tops out below that.
- Cathy Waterman's verified enamel earrings at $3,200 are 2.16× YÀN's top. Alice Cicolini's mid is 1.74× YÀN's top.

**Caveats and nuance the brand owner should weigh:**
1. **Provenance + maker-recognition discount.** Masriera carries 187 years of continuous brand equity; YÀN is months old in the market. A 30–50% pricing discount vs Masriera at the same craft tier is defensible at this stage. That still puts a defensible YÀN top SKU at **$2,500–$3,500**, not $1,479.
2. **CN production discount.** Yunnan workshop labor + materials are genuinely cheaper than Barcelona/Lisbon/NYC ateliers. A 40% structural discount is fair. That STILL implies YÀN top should be in the **$2,800–$3,400 range** if the technique is truly comparable plique-à-jour quality.
3. **Risk of underpricing as a quality signal.** At $1,479 a buyer comparing to Masriera at $5,690 may reasonably assume "the cheap one must not be real plique-à-jour" — undermining the rarity claim that is YÀN's strongest brand asset.
4. **Where YÀN's entry pricing is reasonable:** if entry SKUs ($179–$500) are cloisonné / champlevé / simple painted enamel — i.e. NOT plique-à-jour — the entry pricing is defensible against Anna e Alex / Loquet ladybird-tier comps. The problem is concentrated at the plique-à-jour SKUs.

**Recommendation (research-side, not strategic):** the plique-à-jour pieces specifically deserve a price floor closer to **$1,800–$2,500** as a first move, with a top SKU pushed toward **$3,000–$3,800**. This still keeps YÀN at a **35–50% discount vs Masriera**, preserves the indie/discovery brand position, but stops signaling "not real plique." Cloisonné, champlevé, and painted-enamel entries can stay where they are.
