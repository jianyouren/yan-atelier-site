- **Brands scanned:** 10 (Catbird US · WWAKE US · Mejuri US/CA · Loquet UK · Foundrae US · Alighieri UK · Pippa Small UK · Charlotte Chesnais FR · Sarah & Sebastian AU · Marco Bicego IT)

Every line below is a real SKU title + real price actually displayed; nothing is "starting at" PR copy.

## Convergent gift-range bands (across all brands)

After triangulating the actual entry SKUs, the published gift filters, and the densest mid-tier inventory:

- **Impulse gift band: ~$30–$160 USD.** Alighieri talismans ($38–$140), Catbird stacking ring / single hoops ($68–$108), Mejuri Satellite ($158), WWAKE single earring ($140), Sarah & Sebastian silver carabiner ($99). Almost always silver, bronze, vermeil, or the thinnest 10k. Pippa Small + Marco Bicego + Loquet + Foundrae + Charlotte Chesnais **do not compete here at all.**
- **Curated gift band: ~$200–$500 USD.** This is where the largest cross-brand density sits: Catbird Snippet/Dewdrop/Cygnet ($298–$468), Mejuri's solid-gold workhorses ($248–$498), WWAKE Sapphire Mist / Diamond Linn / Caged Pearl Ring ($248–$490), Alighieri Vessel of Offerings Earrings (£395 ≈ $500), Pippa Small entry amulet (£500 ≈ $633), Foundrae's cheapest pendant ($400), Loquet's cheapest charm (£280 ≈ $354), Charlotte Chesnais ear cuffs ($291–$360). **This is THE indie-fine gift sweet spot.**
- **Premium gift band: ~$500–$1,500 USD.** Loquet pavé/heart charms (£500–£700), Pippa Small mid amulets/studs (£570–£990), Foundrae Reverie Baby ($1,900 sits just outside), Charlotte Chesnais vermeil signature pieces ($589–$1,180), WWAKE Caged Pearl Bracelet / Tempo pendant ($515–$650+), Mejuri's diamond toppers ($498–$648). The "serious gift / self-purchase decision" band.
- **Indie luxury / collector band: ~$1,500–$7,000 USD.** Pippa Small opal/ruby tier (£2,000–£7,770), Foundrae signature medallions ($1,900–$6,850), Charlotte Chesnais solid-gold tier ($1,700–$6,435), Alighieri solid gold/diamond ($595–£3,500), Loquet 18K lockets (£1,600–£8,800), Marco Bicego's **floor** ($2,310–$6,800). WWAKE atelier pieces ($5,180–$9,665).
- **Above ~$7k** is no longer "indie fine gift" — it's bridal / collector / one-of-one. Pippa Small Shway Cuff (£22,650), Foundrae Grande Dame ($35,000), WWAKE Black Opal ($9,575+) live here.

## Key signals observed

1. **Enamel as a craft category is essentially absent from this 10-brand panel.** Only Foundrae publicly uses enamel (sigils + ceramic-enamel), and they price the cheapest enamel SKU at $2,400 (Vivacity Baby Medallion). Loquet does painted miniature charms but not fired plique. Nobody is doing fired plique-à-jour in the indie commerce tier — YÀN has **no direct enamel-craft competitor in this panel**. The auction market is the right pricing reference (already covered in `plique_pricing_evidence_2026-06-23.md`).
2. **Made-in-China vs made-in-US/EU/AU positioning impact:** None of these brands hide origin, but Mejuri/Catbird/Foundrae lean hard on "Brooklyn-made" / "recycled US gold" / Sustainable narrative. Marco Bicego = Vicenza, Italy and prices accordingly (+100% over Foundrae for similar gold weight, just on geographic prestige). **YÀN's Yunnan + Master-Enameller story has untapped pricing power IF documented to Western-buyer-expected standards** (artisan name, place, fire temp, hours-of-work — all of which YÀN's Craft page already does).
3. **Silver vs gold matters enormously at the floor; matters less at the top:**
   - Brands with a vermeil/silver entry (Mejuri, Sarah & Sebastian, Alighieri, Catbird singles) can offer **$58–$160** entry.
   - Brands that are gold-only (Pippa Small, Loquet 18K, Marco Bicego, Foundrae solid-gold tier) have **floors $400–$2,310**.
   - **YÀN's choice to price-entry around ¥680 ($94) without a silver line means YÀN is currently competing on price floor against silver/vermeil brands — for a fired plique-à-jour piece.** This is the strongest underpricing signal in the data.
4. **"Gift" filters are publicly published by only 2 of 10:** Catbird (Under $200/$300/$500), Sarah & Sebastian (SOLDERED experience cards at fixed AUD breakpoints). **The under-$300 / under-$500 filter is a proven UX pattern for gift JTBD** — this is also where Loquet, Foundrae, and Mejuri funnel buyers via their PR, even without explicit filters. YÀN's Gift JTBD would map to: under-¥1,500 / under-¥3,000 / under-¥5,000.
5. **Single-earring SKUs as a hidden price-floor trick:** Catbird, WWAKE, Foundrae all sell singles to publish a sub-$150 price point on a craft piece without actually delivering a pair at that price. This is worth knowing — it's not deception, it's a known indie pricing pattern.
6. **Marco Bicego is the wrong heritage comp.** Italian solid-gold-only with no piece under $1,900. YÀN is NOT in Marco Bicego's tier and shouldn't be benchmarked there. The right Western heritage comp for YÀN is **Foundrae** (sigil enamel craft, indie-luxury position, US-made narrative) — and Foundrae's $400 floor / $2,400 enamel floor / $1,900 medallion floor is the strongest evidence point in this report.
7. **Pippa Small's £90 "trinket" entry is the only sub-£500 piece on her catalog** — the rest sits £500–£22,650. This is the "narrative-Pippa" model: one cheap hook, real catalog floor is much higher. YÀN should consider if its sub-$200 entry is doing the same hook job, or if it's actually getting bought as the median piece (signal: order mix).
