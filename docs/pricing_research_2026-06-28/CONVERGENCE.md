# Pricing Research Convergence · 2026-06-28

User question (paraphrased): "are we too expensive vs gift-site prices?" Answer based on 3 parallel agents reading real catalog + auction + cross-border data.

## The short answer

**You are not too expensive. You are mispriced in the OPPOSITE direction across most of the catalog.** Specifically:

- 🟢 **Top plique-à-jour SKUs ($1,479) are 4× under-priced vs the only living direct peer.**
- 🟡 **Mid tier ($369–$899) is correctly priced.** Hold.
- 🔴 **Entry tier ($179) is cost-negative under current 2.0× markup** — shipping eats 64% of the markup. Either raise floor or kill the entry tier.

The "礼品站太贵" worry stems from the wrong reference group. YÀN is benchmarking against Mejuri/Catbird mentally, but the actual peer set for plique-à-jour craft is Masriera / Lalique-archive secondary — a register where YÀN is the cheap option.

## Three lenses, three convergent verdicts

### Lens 1 · International indie gift-range (`01_intl_indie.md`)
- Convergent bands in the panel of Catbird / WWAKE / Mejuri / Loquet / Foundrae / Alighieri / Pippa Small / Chesnais / Sarah & Sebastian / Marco Bicego:
  - **Impulse gift**: $30–$160 (silver/vermeil; no enamel competitors)
  - **Curated gift**: $200–$500 (densest cluster)
  - **Premium gift**: $500–$1,500
  - **Indie luxury**: $1,500–$7,000
- YÀN $179–$1,479 lands "competitive-to-cheap":
  - Entry $179 → **under-priced** vs anyone doing fired plique-à-jour
  - Mid → sweet spot
  - Top $1,479 → **floor of indie luxury, under Marco Bicego entry**
- **Foundrae is the ONLY direct enamel comp in the indie-craft set; cheapest enamel SKU = $2,400.**

### Lens 2 · Enamel + plique-à-jour specialist (`02_enamel_specialist.md`)
- **Masriera (Spain, 1839–present, only living direct plique workshop):**
  - Entry plique-à-jour SKU: **$5,690 retail / $3,983 sale**
  - YÀN top $1,479 = **26% of Masriera entry**
- **Auction ceiling (sets the historical reference):**
  - Lalique pendant: **£53,740** (Bonhams 2023)
  - Vever butterfly brooch: **$27,720** (Rago 2024)
  - Lalique Cattleya set: **€738,500** (Sotheby's Paris)
- Agent recommendation: **plique-only repricing to $1,800–$2,500 floor / $3,000–$3,800 top.** Cloisonné/painted-enamel entries stay.

### Lens 3 · CN cross-border peers + markup math (`03_cn_crossborder_markup.md`)
- TO SUMMER / DOCUMENTS / YVMIN CN→intl ratio converges **1.2–1.7× (median 1.4×)**. YÀN's flat 2.0× looks higher than peer norm.
- BUT — peers achieve the lower ratio by **not absorbing DDP** (customer pays duties). YÀN absorbs DDP. Can't apples-to-apples compare.
- Tier-by-tier math reality check:
  - **Mid $369–$899**: 2.0× **right** — covers DDP (20-22% EU) + shipping ($50-60) + Stripe intl (5.9% + 2% FX, not 3.4% the brief assumed) + healthy margin
  - **Entry $179**: **2.0× insufficient** — fixed shipping cost eats 64% of markup; loss-leader at best
  - **Top $1,479**: 2.0× could theoretically flatten to 1.7-1.8× but **shouldn't** — top SKU anchors brand register; lowering price dilutes positioning

## Recommended price-architecture move (NOT executed — pending user decision)

⚠ Per `feedback_pricing_wait_for_user_table_2026-06-25` Claude must not auto-propose ¥. Below is the **decision framework + ranges**, not a ship list. User holds the call.

### A. Plique-à-jour SKUs (currently ¥3,800 / $1,074 mid, ¥5,180 / $1,479 top)
**Convergent recommendation: raise the plique floor + ceiling.**
- Plique floor: $1,800-$2,500 retail → CNY ~¥6,300-¥8,750
- Plique ceiling: $3,000-$3,800 retail → CNY ~¥10,500-¥13,300
- Defensibility: only living direct peer (Masriera) sits 2-3× above; auction archive sits 20×+ above. Anchor copy: "Bonhams Lalique pendant 2023 £53,740 / Rago Vever brooch 2024 $27,720" already on the Craft page.
- Risk: user just bumped these ¥2,880 → ¥3,800 on 2026-06-23. Another raise within 5 days is operationally jumpy. Possible to phase: $1,800 floor now, $3,000+ when first sale lands.

### B. Cloisonné mid-tier ($369-$899)
**Hold.** Mid is the right price. Convergent across all 3 lenses.

### C. Entry tier ($179, currently ¥680)
**Two valid paths:**
- **Path 1 — Raise entry floor to ~$249-$299** (~¥870-¥1,050). Brings entry into the "curated gift" band where the densest competitor cluster sits. Covers real cost. Loses the "impulse gift" reach but YÀN's atelier register doesn't fit the impulse band anyway.
- **Path 2 — Kill entry tier, start at $369.** Cleaner brand-register move. Removes the cost-negative SKU. Tracks the Foundrae enamel-floor-at-$2,400 philosophy. Most editorial-tier indie brands don't sell sub-$300.
- **Anti-pattern (do not do)**: keep ¥680 entry AND keep 2.0× markup AND eat shipping. You lose money on every sale at that SKU.

### D. Currency-by-currency check
After the multi-currency ship today, verify rounding hits sensible thresholds in each currency:
- $179 → £141 → €166 → A$269. The A$269 looks ESPECIALLY weak in AUD luxury-gift register (where buyers expect AUD 350+ for indie fine). EUR/GBP also look low for European fine-jewelry register.
- If entry stays low for any reason, consider per-region floor (AUD floor 349, EUR floor 219 etc.) — but this is operational complexity; per-SKU ¥-based markup is cleaner.

## What is NOT recommended (despite cargo-cult temptation)

- ❌ **Lowering prices to match Mejuri/Catbird** — different category. Mejuri is mass-vermeil. Foundrae is the editorial-enamel comp at $2,400+.
- ❌ **Flat 1.4× markup matching CN peers** — those peers don't absorb DDP. Apples to oranges.
- ❌ **Top SKU at $5,000+ to match Masriera retail** — too early; brand has no time-in-market trust signal yet. Stay 25-50% under Masriera for first 12 months, raise after first 10+ sales.
