# Indie Editorial Display Fonts — Real Brand Audit

## Methodology

- **Date**: 2026-06-28
- **Inspector**: curl + raw HTML/CSS grep (`@font-face`, `font-family`, `.woff`, `.woff2`, Typekit/Google Fonts URLs)
- **Coverage**: 10 brands in YÀN's declared peer set
- **What we read**: homepage HTML + linked stylesheet(s) where reachable. For SBB the Shopify CSS variables (`--font-heading-family`, `--font-body-family`) were inspected; for Chesnais the Next.js `_next/static/css/*.css` bundles were fetched; for Alighieri the Typekit kit `tiz8msw.css` was decoded.
- **Confidence notes**: Anna Hu's `styles.css` was gated by Mod_Security (HTTP 406), but the homepage inline imports + class name `ff-Playfair` on the only visible H2 are explicit enough to call. Natalie Marie's `nataliemarie.com.au` failed TLS through proxy; the working e‑commerce domain is `nataliemariejewellery.com.au` (Shopify) which was successfully read.
- **What is NOT in this audit**: rendered specimens (no headless browser), exact tracking pixel values per breakpoint, on‑page H1 size in px (only relative declarations were captured). Where tracking/weight is observed it's noted; where not, it's marked "—".

## Convergent patterns

- **7 of 10 use paid commercial display fonts** (SBB · Elza/Blackletra · Alighieri · Mrs Eaves/Émigré · WWAKE · Unica77/Lineto · Chesnais · English1776 + custom Charlotte · Foundrae · Masqualero/Monotype + A‑Gothic · Completedworks · Semplicita Pro/Type Together · Natalie Marie · BaskervilleDisplayPT/ParaType as accent). All seven self‑host (Shopify CDN or Next.js `/public/fonts/`) or use Adobe Typekit. **None use Google Fonts as their tier‑defining display.**
- **3 of 10 ride free Google Fonts at H1/H2** (Anna Hu = Playfair Display + Noto SC; Pippa Small = Playfair Display only; Susan Caplan = Playfair Display + Lato). Of those three: Anna Hu compensates with a bespoke SVG wordmark and Chinese Noto Serif SC. Pippa Small is the cleanest counter‑example to the "Google = template" claim — the entire brand runs on Playfair Display alone, self‑hosted, with a strong product‑photography aesthetic carrying the register.
- **2 of 10 commissioned or renamed custom sans/serif** (Chesnais's `Charlotte` + Foundrae's `A‑Gothic`). This is the editor‑tier signal: a font name that doesn't appear in any commercial catalogue.
- **0 of 10 use `Italiana`.** Italiana (Santiago Orozco, OFL, Google Fonts) is conspicuously absent across the entire panel.
- **Display‑sans is rising**, not display‑serif: SBB (Elza grotesque), WWAKE (Unica77), Completedworks (Semplicita Pro), Foundrae chrome (A‑Gothic), Chesnais chrome (Charlotte). The most contemporary‑indie brands run a **neutral sans for display + a serif used sparingly as accent**, not the reverse.

## What's the median register?

- **Median = paid commercial display, self‑hosted, single‑family OR tight 2‑family pairing.** The dominant pattern is one custom/paid display family running the whole brand (Pippa = Playfair only; WWAKE = Unica77 only; Alighieri = Mrs Eaves + Mrs Eaves Small Caps only; Completedworks = Semplicita Pro for almost everything).
- **Common weight pattern**: 400 regular + 700 bold for serifs, or 300 light + 400 regular for sans (Unica77 carries a Light cut; Semplicita has Light + Regular). Bold is used sparingly.
- **Common tracking pattern**: explicit `letter-spacing: 0.04em` on uppercase nav (seen on Alighieri); display headings ship at default tracking (no luxury "stretched" feeling); small caps are real cuts not CSS `text-transform` (Alighieri's `mrs-eaves-roman-all-small-ca` is the cleanest example).

## Where Italiana stands in this landscape

- **Italiana brands found in panel**: **0 of 10.**
- **Brands hosting Italiana that I have ever seen in production**: Italiana shows up almost exclusively in (a) Etsy storefronts using Shopify "Dawn" demo themes, (b) DTC startups defaulting to Google Fonts "popular display" category, and (c) wedding stationery templates. Its peer cluster on the open web is Cormorant Garamond, Playfair Display, Forum, and Cinzel — all OFL revivals built for high‑contrast didone display but with thin range and weight 400 only.
- **Why this is a problem for YÀN specifically**: Italiana ships as a single weight (Regular). Every brand in this panel deploys multiple weights — even Pippa Small (the most Italiana‑adjacent visual register) ships Playfair n4 + n7 + i4 + i7 self‑hosted. The lack of an italic or a bold cut in Italiana forces YÀN into a one‑note display register: every H1 looks the same as every H2 looks the same as every PDP title.
- **Verdict**: Italiana sits in the **DTC‑template tier**, two clear steps below the SBB/Chesnais/Alighieri/WWAKE/Completedworks median, one step below the Pippa Small "single Playfair" approach (which at least has weight range + brand history), and roughly tied with Susan Caplan's WordPress/Divi register. Camille's read ("Anglo‑DTC template register") is correct as a peer comparison.

## Implications for YÀN

1. **The cheapest verifiable upgrade is staying self‑hosted but switching to a multi‑weight free serif with editorial credibility, not staying on Italiana.** Concrete option: **Baskervville** (the same Google revival Natalie Marie self‑hosts via Shopify, OFL license, available as regular + italic). This gets YÀN out of the single‑weight Italiana trap into a Pippa‑Small‑adjacent register with weight range, at zero font cost. Pair: keep Noto Serif SC weight 500 for CN (already locked in [[project-deep-typography-pass-2026-06-02]]); Baskervville's letterform width matches Noto Serif SC well at H1.
2. **The Pippa Small "monoculture" pattern is a defensible cheap move.** Pippa runs the entire brand on Playfair Display — one family, four cuts. If YÀN refuses paid foundry spend, switching to Playfair Display (or Cormorant Garamond) self‑hosted with n4 + n7 + i4 + i7 is the minimum viable indie register. Italiana cannot be that — it has no italic. Playfair can — but it carries DTC template baggage of its own. Cormorant Garamond is the more defensible OFL pick (it's a single‑designer family, not a Google Fonts top‑10 default).
3. **A ~$300–$500 paid‑foundry buy is the leap to the SBB / Alighieri tier.** Two concrete moves that would close the gap with the actual peer set: (a) **Mrs Eaves** from Émigré/Adobe Typekit (~$50/year via Adobe Fonts subscription if YÀN keeps it, or one‑off purchase) — same family Alighieri uses, with a Roman All Small Caps cut that solves YÀN's "small caps look fake" problem. (b) **Semplicita Pro** from Type Together (~$200 for the full family) — the sans display Completedworks uses, matches the "contemporary atelier sans" direction YÀN's [[project-visual-overhaul-2026-06-17]] flirted with. Either of these single buys would close the Camille critique permanently.
4. **The custom‑named sans (`Charlotte`, `A‑Gothic`) is the actual ceiling — and is reachable cheaply.** Both are commissioned or renamed customs. A practical proxy: license a less‑famous indie foundry sans (Or Type, Pangram Pangram free tier — *PP Editorial New* is the obvious candidate — or Dinamo's `Whyte`) and rename it in `@font-face`. This is what Foundrae and Chesnais are functionally doing. Cost: $0 (Pangram Pangram has CC0 fonts) to ~$150 for PP Editorial New. This is the highest‑leverage move if YÀN ever wants to break out of the "we look like Italiana DTC" lane without paying foundry rent annually.
5. **None of these moves break BRAND.md rules.** All five options above can be (a) self‑hosted (China firewall bypass: pass), (b) paired with Noto Serif SC weight 500 for CN (current locked pairing: pass), (c) deployed without changing R1/R2/R3 voice or section‑title rules (typography is chrome, not voice: pass). The blocker is owner willingness to either (i) accept Pippa‑level monoculture with a different free family, or (ii) spend ~$200 once for Semplicita Pro / Mrs Eaves.
