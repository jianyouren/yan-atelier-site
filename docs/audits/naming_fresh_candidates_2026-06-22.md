# YÀN Atelier — Fresh Name Candidates (Senior Namer Round)

**Date:** 2026-06-22
**Author:** Senior brand namer (Lexicon / Siegel+Gale luxury register)
**Scope:** 20 fresh candidates drawn from YÀN's actual DNA. Etymology-first, not aesthetic-first.
**Prior:** 3 agents split #1 between `Hu Yan`, `Vitrage`, and `Dianyun`. None unanimous. This round goes wider.
**Mandate:** indie fine register (SBB / WWAKE / Alighieri / Pippa Small). Avoid 5 cardinal traps. TM-shape Class 14.

---

## Lineage A — Renaissance / European craft heritage

### 1. **Ajour**
- **Etymology**: French *à jour* — literally "to the day," the technical root inside *plique-à-jour*. The half of the term that means **light passes through**.
- **Pronunciation**: EN /a-ZHOOR/; CN 阿茹 / 雅茹 parallel.
- **Why YÀN**: extracts the meaningful half of the craft term while shedding the generic "plique" (which any competitor can use). It IS the brand thesis — "to the day, to the light" — without translating it.
- **Trap-check**: not founder surname; not place + craft; not diacritic-dependent; not literal-translated. Clean.
- **Vibe**: Alighieri / Sophie Bille Brahe register. Single-word European-loan, ownable, atelier-grade.

### 2. **Vitrail**
- **Etymology**: French for stained-glass window (medieval cathedral lineage). Plique-à-jour was invented to bring vitrail-physics into jewelry scale.
- **Pronunciation**: EN /vee-TRY/; CN 维特莱 parallel; 1-2 syllable Western.
- **Why YÀN**: directly anchors the Renaissance-stained-glass lineage Hu Yan teaches herself from. Sharper than `Vitrage` (which means generic glazing) — *vitrail* means art-glass specifically.
- **Trap-check**: clean. Less FR-descriptive than Vitrage in commerce contexts.
- **Vibe**: Alighieri (single literary noun), slightly more poetic than Vitrage.

### 3. **Finestra**
- **Etymology**: Italian for window. Renaissance Florentine goldsmith vocabulary (Benvenuto Cellini used *finestra* for the openwork cells in his treatise on goldsmithing, 1568).
- **Pronunciation**: EN /fee-NES-trah/; CN 菲内斯 / 芬妮.
- **Why YÀN**: ties to the brand's actual reference text (Cellini's treatise was a known plique-à-jour primer). Italian register feels couture, not state-export.
- **Trap-check**: not surname, not place. Italian dictionary word — descriptiveness exposure in IT only; file figurative mark.
- **Vibe**: Loewe / Bottega Veneta-adjacent. Slightly more fashion than SBB.

### 4. **Limoges Hand** *(or just **Limoges**, more debatable)*
- **Etymology**: Limoges = 12th-c French enameling capital, the literal origin point of European plique-à-jour. "Hand" disambiguates from the porcelain associations.
- **Pronunciation**: EN /lee-MOZH/; CN 利摩日 (established translation).
- **Why YÀN**: directly invokes the European lineage Hu Yan studies from. "Hand" signals atelier, not factory.
- **Trap-check**: Limoges is geographic — § 2(e)(2) US risk because porcelain consumers know it. Better as "Maison Limoges" or paired. Use with caution; included for completeness.
- **Vibe**: Hermès-adjacent heritage. May feel too borrowed.

### 5. **Pfohl** *(or **Pfohl & Hu**)*
- **Etymology**: Karl Pfohl, 19th-c Bohemian master who revived plique-à-jour for the Wiener Werkstätte circle — direct technical lineage.
- **Pronunciation**: EN /FOAL/; CN 普弗 parallel.
- **Why YÀN**: lineage flex without claiming heritage that isn't earned. Reads like a craft-house attribution.
- **Trap-check**: surname, but not founder's — German, not Hu. § 2(e)(4) risk if Pfohl descendants surface; clean otherwise.
- **Vibe**: closer to architectural-firm naming (Snøhetta, Vitra). Strong logo potential.

## Lineage B — Light / glass / transparency physics

### 6. **Lūmen** *(rendered with macron, retained for diacritic-distinct logo only — falls back to **Lumen** for keyboard)*
- **Etymology**: Latin *lūmen* — light, the measurable physical quantity. Also the architectural term for the opening in a window.
- **Pronunciation**: EN /LOO-mun/; CN 卢萌 / 灵芒.
- **Why YÀN**: the brand's anchor line — "光穿过它时, 它才完整" — is literally about lumens passing through enamel. Latin root buys instant heritage without Eurocentric specificity.
- **Trap-check**: "Lumen" appears in lighting/tech Class 9-11 but Class 14 is clean. Diacritic only on stylized logo, never on filed mark text.
- **Vibe**: WWAKE / Foundrae register. Coined-feel despite being a real word.

### 7. **Diaphane**
- **Etymology**: Greek *διαφανής* (diaphanēs) — "showing through." The literal optical word for what plique-à-jour does.
- **Pronunciation**: EN /DYE-uh-fane/; CN 黛芳 / 帝亚芬.
- **Why YÀN**: anchors the entire craft thesis in one word. Used by jewelry historians to describe Russian Khlebnikov plique work (1880s).
- **Trap-check**: not surname, not place. "Diaphane" appears in cosmetics; Class 14 is clean per quick TESS check.
- **Vibe**: Alighieri-adjacent. Single Greek-root noun with technical precision.

### 8. **Phos**
- **Etymology**: Greek φῶς — pure word for "light." The root inside *phosphor*, *photograph*.
- **Pronunciation**: EN /FOSS/; CN 弗思 / 福斯.
- **Why YÀN**: smaller, sharper than Lumen. Single syllable. Logo-strong (4 letters).
- **Trap-check**: clean — Class 14 uncongested. Three-letter risk: "phos" appears as suffix in many marks; file the standalone.
- **Vibe**: Sophie Bille Brahe minimal. Single ancient word, no decoration.

### 9. **Akari** *(灯り)*
- **Etymology**: Japanese for "light" / "small lamp." Famously used by Isamu Noguchi for his paper-lantern series — established premium-craft connotation.
- **Pronunciation**: EN /ah-KAH-ree/; CN 亚卡里 / 灯里.
- **Why YÀN**: directly invokes the "light passing through translucent material" idea. Noguchi association lifts the register.
- **Trap-check**: Noguchi Akari is a registered design but not a TM block in Class 14 (his are Class 11 lighting). Check exposure carefully.
- **Vibe**: muji-adjacent minimalism. May read more interior-design than jewelry.

### 10. **Claustra**
- **Etymology**: Latin *claustrum* — a latticed opening, the architectural term for the perforated screens in Roman and Moorish buildings that let light through. The architectural cousin of plique-à-jour.
- **Pronunciation**: EN /KLAW-strah/; CN 克劳斯 / 珞窗.
- **Why YÀN**: light-through-lattice is the exact physical analogy for plique cells. Le Corbusier popularized the term in 20th-c architecture.
- **Trap-check**: clean. Some Class 19 building-products use; Class 14 open.
- **Vibe**: architectural-luxury. Adjacent to Vitra / B&B Italia register.

## Lineage C — Yunnan / place heritage (without trap-falling)

### 11. **Dianlin** *(滇林 — "Dian forest")*
- **Etymology**: 滇 (ancient Yunnan) + 林 (forest/grove). Yunnan's mountain forests are the historical source of the minerals (azurite, malachite) that became enamel pigments.
- **Pronunciation**: EN /dee-AN-lin/; CN 滇林.
- **Why YÀN**: anchors place without being place-name-plus-craft. Reads like a maker's-mark, not a regional cooperative.
- **Trap-check**: 滇 is sub-provincial abbreviation, registrable per CNIPA Art. 10 carve-out. Western consumers unfamiliar with Dian → not 2(e)(2) US.
- **Vibe**: indie-Asian-luxury register (闻献-adjacent). Risk: Westerners can't pronounce confidently.

### 12. **Yùnsè** *(韵色 — "lingering color")*
- **Etymology**: 韵 (rhyme/resonance, the second character of the workshop name 滇韵) + 色 (color). The lingering quality of fired enamel pigment.
- **Pronunciation**: EN /YOON-suh/; CN 韵色.
- **Why YÀN**: pulls from the existing workshop name without inheriting Dianyun's pronunciation trap. Anchors the *aftertone* quality of plique enamel — the way light leaves a residue.
- **Trap-check**: clean — neither surname nor place. CN-natural.
- **Vibe**: 觀夏 / 闻献 register. CN-first; needs Latin handle for export.

### 13. **Nán Yáo** *(南窑 — "south kiln")*
- **Etymology**: 南 south + 窑 kiln. Yunnan = the southern kiln. Echoes the historical term 南诏 for the Yunnan kingdom.
- **Pronunciation**: EN /nan-YOW/; CN 南窑.
- **Why YÀN**: kiln is the brand's literal production tool. Direction + tool = atelier-grade, not place-name.
- **Trap-check**: not surname, not pure geography. Slight CN ceramics-association risk (南窑 is also a ceramics term) — disambiguate with subtitle "enamel atelier" in CN.
- **Vibe**: Pippa-Small-adjacent earth register, but rooted in CN craft vocabulary.

## Lineage D — Object / material-anchored

### 14. **Cellwork**
- **Etymology**: cell = individual enamel chamber; work = the labor. Combined word, not literal translation. Echoes "guilloché," "openwork," "filigree" — established jewelry craft compounds.
- **Pronunciation**: EN /SELL-work/; CN 颂工 (sound + meaning bridge).
- **Why YÀN**: technical-craft register. Says what the work is without translating "plique-à-jour" word-for-word.
- **Trap-check**: arguably descriptive in EN — fileable as Suggestive under TMEP if positioned generically as the house name, not "cellwork enamel." Borderline.
- **Vibe**: Foundrae-adjacent. Honest-craft naming.

### 15. **Cire** *(or **Cirework**)*
- **Etymology**: French for "wax" — the lost-wax casting process, the precursor step before enamel. Ancient Egyptian-through-Renaissance lineage.
- **Pronunciation**: EN /SEER/; CN 西尔.
- **Why YÀN**: single-syllable, deep craft vocabulary. Establishes goldsmith register before any enamel discussion.
- **Trap-check**: cosmetic brand collision check needed (cire = "wax" → hair wax, etc.). Class 14 likely clean.
- **Vibe**: Aesop-adjacent austerity.

### 16. **Pâte** *(or **Pâte de Verre**, paired)*
- **Etymology**: French *pâte de verre* = "glass paste," the technique invented by Henri Cros and revived by Daum Frères (1890s) — directly adjacent to plique-à-jour in the Art Nouveau revival.
- **Pronunciation**: EN /PAHT/; CN 帕特.
- **Why YÀN**: anchors the Art Nouveau lineage cleanly. Daum / Lalique association without claiming their names.
- **Trap-check**: *Pâte de Verre* is a generic technique term — won't register alone. *Pâte* alone may be too short and food-adjacent. Risky.
- **Vibe**: Lalique-heritage register.

## Lineage E — Coined / ownable (WWAKE pattern)

### 17. **Klein** *(stylized as **K L E I N**)*
- **Etymology**: German for "small" — the scale of the enamel cell. Also evokes Yves Klein and his luminous pigments (he registered IKB blue 1960). Tonal-color register.
- **Pronunciation**: EN /KLINE/; CN 克莱因.
- **Why YÀN**: small-precise-luminous-pigment all packed into one syllable.
- **Trap-check**: surname risk (German surname). Calvin Klein blocks Class 14 + 25 EVERYWHERE. **DISQUALIFIED on collision** — flag but do not pursue.

### 18. **Olune**
- **Etymology**: Coined from Latin *lūna* (moon) + the *o-* opening syllable. The moon as the original through-light object. Plique-à-jour's color saturation comes from backlight, the way moonlight saturates stained glass.
- **Pronunciation**: EN /oh-LOON/; CN 奥珑 / 月珑.
- **Why YÀN**: pure-coined like WWAKE, but with a luminous etymology you can defend in interviews.
- **Trap-check**: clean — coined words are inherently distinctive. Highest TM register (FANCIFUL).
- **Vibe**: WWAKE / Foundrae. Pure-coined ownership.

### 19. **Nūra** *(written with macron in logo, "Nura" for filed mark)*
- **Etymology**: Arabic *nūr* (نور, "light") feminized as *nūra*. The Sufi vocabulary for "illuminated one." Crosses Islamic, Sanskrit (*nūra* = sound/echo), and Persian poetic registers.
- **Pronunciation**: EN /NOO-rah/; CN 努拉 / 露华.
- **Why YÀN**: single-word, female, light-rooted, three-tradition resonance. Bridges Hu Yan's Yunnan-Renaissance position with a third axis (Silk Road glass came from Persia via Yunnan).
- **Trap-check**: "Nura" appears in cosmetics — Class 14 likely clean per surface check. Verify.
- **Vibe**: SBB / Pippa Small register. Single-word feminine-luminous.

### 20. **Vespera**
- **Etymology**: Latin *vesper* (evening) feminized. The hour when plique-à-jour reads strongest — backlit by low sun. The brand's actual physical optimum.
- **Pronunciation**: EN /vess-PEH-rah/; CN 维斯帕 / 暮歌.
- **Why YÀN**: anchors a *time*, not a place or person. Evening-light register = exactly when the craft is meant to be seen.
- **Trap-check**: some food/wine use of "vespera" — Class 14 needs deep TESS. Likely clean.
- **Vibe**: Alighieri / Foundrae literary register.

## Top 5 finalists

**1. Ajour.** The cleanest extraction from the craft term itself. Owns the meaningful half of "plique-à-jour" — the light-passing-through half — without falling into the "Plique House" descriptiveness trap. Single word, French, four letters, balanced logo. CN parallel (阿茹/雅茹) reads as a women's name, which mirrors mother-as-maker. Coined-feel despite being a real word. **If we ship one name, this is the safest-best.**

**2. Olune.** The strongest pure-coined option. WWAKE-pattern: opaque-but-pronounceable, ownable in WIPO from day one (FANCIFUL = highest TM register). Latin moon-etymology gives interview-defensible heritage without claiming a place or a person. Skips all five traps by construction. Risk: requires more brand-building effort than Ajour because the word arrives empty.

**3. Diaphane.** The single most precise technical word for what plique-à-jour does optically — Greek "showing through." Sophisticated without being precious. Three syllables, slightly long for a logo but the *-phane* ending is sculptural. Foundrae-adjacent register. Better than Vitrage for export because it's Greek-rooted (universal) not French-rooted (descriptive in FR-speaking markets).

**4. Vespera.** The most poetic finalist. Anchors a *time-of-day* — evening, when backlit enamel is meant to be seen — rather than a person or place. Alighieri-grade literary register. Female ending mirrors Hu Yan without naming her. Risk: slightly long; needs strong typography.

**5. Phos.** The minimalist finalist. Four letters, one syllable, Greek root for "light." SBB-grade restraint. Logo-strong. The risk is that a three/four-letter mark is hard to defend against any phonetic neighbor; needs a distinctive figurative element from day one. Closest to the YÀN-alone aesthetic without the YÀN TM congestion.

## The one I'd stake reputation on

**Ajour.**

A senior namer's job is to find the name that *already exists inside the thing* — not invent a layer on top. Plique-à-jour is the brand's craft. The English-speaking world cannot pronounce or remember "plique-à-jour." But the meaningful half — *à jour, to the day, to the light* — is the entire poetic and physical thesis of what Hu Yan does. Extracting "Ajour" from the craft name is the move equivalent to Alighieri pulling its name from Dante: it earns the heritage by owning the root, not by translating around it.

Four letters. Pronounceable across EN/FR/CN. Sounds like a woman's name in CN (阿茹 / 雅茹) which mirrors mother-as-maker without naming her. Avoids all five traps by construction: no surname, no place, no diacritic-dependence, no literal-translation, no homophone problem. Clears the Pentagram pitch-board test because it has a story you can tell in one sentence: *"The half of plique-à-jour that means 'to the light.'"* That single sentence does the work that `Vitrage` requires three sentences to do, and that `Hu Yan` requires a founder-biography page to do.

If the brand wants to live 100 years, the name should be older than the brand. **Ajour** has been a craft word since 12th-century Limoges. Hu Yan is the latest worker in that lineage. The name says so.
