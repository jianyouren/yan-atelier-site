# YÀN Atelier — Naming Brand-Architecture Audit

**Date:** 2026-06-22
**Author:** Brand strategy (Wolff Olins / Pentagram register)
**Scope:** Architecture only. Not legal (TM cleared separately 2026-06-04). Not SEO.

---

## Naming-pattern landscape

Indie fine jewelry above the $300–$3,000 AOV line clusters into **four naming archetypes**, and the success rate of each is wildly uneven.

**A. Founder name (surname-led).** Sophie Bille Brahe, Pippa Small, Anna Hu, FARIS. This is the dominant pattern at the top of the pack. It works because the founder *is* the product — the buyer is buying a hand and a point of view, not a brand operating system. The signal is "this is a person, not a marketing department." Risk: founder-coupled brands cap at the founder's longevity unless a succession story is built in.

**B. Coined word.** WWAKE, Foundrae, Mejuri, Yvmin. Distribution-bimodal: at the indie end (WWAKE, Foundrae) the word is *opaque and ownable*, anchoring a private mood. At the DTC-mass end (Mejuri) it reads disposable. The discriminator is **whether the word resists being decoded** — WWAKE doesn't translate, and that's the point.

**C. Literary / mythological noun.** Alighieri, 闻献 (Documents), 觀夏 (To Summer). High atelier-feel ceiling, low DTC-friendliness. Demands the brand earn the reference — Alighieri's Dante quotes, 觀夏's seasonal essays. Lazy execution kills it instantly.

**D. Descriptive / material naming.** Stone & Strand, Catbird. Reads DTC-friendly, low couture-ceiling. Acceptable for volume plays; ceiling-capped at ~$800 AOV. Wrong room for YÀN.

**Patterns that kill indie luxury:** translated-literal craft terms (`Clearwindow`, `Plique House`), place-name + craft (`YÀN Yunnan`), pinyin-only without a Latin handle (`Dianyun`). These all read like 1990s state-export brands, not 2026 atelier register.

## Top 3 ranked

**1. Hu Yan (full founder name) — 56/70.** This is the SBB / Anna Hu / Pippa Small move. It removes every translation problem at the source: there is nothing to translate, the founder *is* the brand. It also solves the TM congestion problem cleanly — `YÀN` as a standalone word is contested in Class 14; `Hu Yan` as a full personal-name mark is in a much less crowded register. Atelier-feel is maximal because founder-named brands are the genre. The 100-year question becomes a real one (succession), but that's a Hermès-shape problem, not a 0→1 problem. CN reading 胡岩 / 胡焰 needs to be locked, but both read fine.

**2. Vitrage — 50/70.** Strongest pure-coined option. Single-word, French register (which buys instant atelier-feel in the indie fine space), semantically anchored to plique-à-jour without being literal-craft-translated. Logotype potential is high (six letters, balanced, V is a sculptural opening letter). Cost: zero CN translatability — Chinese audience will need a parallel CN name (e.g., 维特拉 / 琉窗), which is real complexity. Best fit if YÀN decides EN-first is the permanent stance.

**3. YÀN (alone) — 47/70.** Single-word founder-derived, FARIS-pattern. Cheapest migration (drop "Atelier", keep everything else). Atelier-feel and logo ownership both jump vs the current `YÀN Atelier` because removing "Atelier" stops over-explaining. Still carries the TM congestion that triggered this whole exercise — so this is the *minimum-change* option, not the *right* option.

---

## The dealbreaker question

> **"In 2036, when a buyer in Paris asks who made this — what is the one word she says back?"**

If the answer is a person, the name is `Hu Yan`. If the answer is a place or a craft, the name is `Vitrage`. If the answer is "I don't know but I love it," the brand has failed regardless of name.

Every other dimension on the scorecard is downstream of this one decision. Founder-named brands and coined-word brands play fundamentally different long games — founder brands build on the maker's biography, coined brands build on the world they construct. YÀN cannot be both.

---

## Closing recommendation

Rename to **`Hu Yan`**. The current `YÀN Atelier` is a transitional name — it explains itself ("Atelier") because it doesn't yet trust itself. Every reference brand above the $1,500 AOV indie line has shed that crutch. `Vitrage` is the safer creative answer and would win on a Pentagram pitch board, but it forces a second decision (CN parallel name) and severs the brand from its single most defensible asset — a real founder, on a real bench, in Yunnan, doing 800°C plique-à-jour that almost no one else does. The Anna Hu / Sophie Bille Brahe / Pippa Small pattern exists because in indie fine jewelry, the founder's name *is* the moat. Stop renting the moat.

**The one trade:** accept that founder-naming caps the exit narrative (no clean acquihire, no second-generation pivot without explicit succession). In exchange you get a name that can never be diluted, never be out-marketed, and never needs a tagline.
