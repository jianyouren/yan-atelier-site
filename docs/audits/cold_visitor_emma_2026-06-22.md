# Emma — Cold Visitor Walkthrough

**Who I am:** Emma, 28, Brooklyn, content marketing. I make $90K, I follow SBB and Mejuri on IG. I just tapped a link from an IG post — pretty enamel butterfly necklace, blue and gold. I expected to land somewhere I could just… buy the thing.

---

## 3-second verdict (literally what I said out loud)

> "Wait — is this a brand or like, an artist's blog?"

Honest read after 3 seconds: pretty butterfly necklace, the headline says *"Wear it. People will ask where you found it."* — cute, but I don't actually know **what** I'd be wearing or what it costs. There's Chinese characters under it. I think… they're in China? Yunnan something. Confidence the brand is "real and shoppable": maybe 40%. It reads more like a tasteful Squarespace portfolio than a store.

---

## First 30 seconds

Eyes go: butterfly necklace photo → headline → little CN line under it → then I scan for a price or a "Shop" button and… don't find one above the fold. Instead there's "Ready to wear · 8 available · 12 made" and "Make it yours · 8–12 weeks." Ok so it's small-batch. Cool. But where's the *grid*? Mejuri shows me 30 things in 2 scrolls. This shows me a vibe.

Trust check: the photo of the butterfly looks real (not AI-glossy), there's one shot of a woman at a torch labeled "Hu Yan, Founder" which helps a lot — like, ok, there's a human. But the footer says **© 2026** which made me do a double-take (am I in the future?). No address, no Instagram link I can see, no "as seen in." It feels handmade in the literal sense — like the website was also handmade.

---

## Browse → product → buy walkthrough

Ok so I scroll, I see "Ready to wear" and I click in expecting a product grid. I get… more vibe sections. Materials strip: 925 silver, 800°C fired enamel, Yunnan handcraft — fine, that's actually a nice detail, 800° sounds legit. But I still don't see **prices** anywhere on the way down.

I finally find products and click one. There's color/material chips which is sweet, but the "Buy" button is basically **"Reserve"** and it opens my email app. Wait, what? I have to **email someone** to buy a $400 necklace? I'm sorry, I was ready to put my Apple Pay in 30 seconds ago. Now I have to compose a sentence to a stranger and wait for a reply? On a Saturday night, in bed, with a glass of wine? No.

There's no Stripe. There's no cart. There's no "Add to bag." The shipping policy / returns links in the footer go nowhere as far as I can tell. The "Gift Finder" sounds promising but… more contact form energy.

**Mobile vs desktop:** Mobile is actually fine — type is readable, photos breathe. Desktop feels slightly *too* spacious, like a museum. Mobile is the better experience honestly.

---

## 5 things that confused / annoyed / made me hesitate

1. **No "Buy Now."** I have to email a Gmail address to order a necklace. That's a 2014 Etsy DM vibe, not 2026 fine jewelry.
2. **Prices are hidden.** I had to dig. If it's $1,400, just say $1,400 — I'll decide if I'm in or out. Hiding it makes me assume it's expensive *and* sketchy.
3. **"© 2026"** in the footer. Tiny thing but I noticed. Also "JP soon," "GL Global" — feels half-built.
4. **No social proof.** No IG link, no press, no reviews, no "shipped to X countries." For a brand asking me to wire money to China sight-unseen, I need *something*.
5. **The headline is cute but doesn't tell me what they sell.** "Wear it. People will ask where you found it." — ok, wear *what*? Necklaces? Earrings? I came from a butterfly post, I shouldn't have to guess the category.

---

## 3 things that hooked me

1. **The butterfly itself is genuinely beautiful.** The plique-à-jour stained-glass look is unlike Mejuri / SBB. If I saw one on someone in Brooklyn I'd ask.
2. **800°C, 5–10 firings, real photo of the maker at a torch.** Specific = credible. That's the one thing that made me believe this isn't dropshipping.
3. **"8 available · 12 made."** Scarcity that feels honest, not manipulative. I like knowing it's not mass-produced.

---

## Would-buy-this-week probability: **8%**

Not because I don't like it — I do — but because **I can't actually check out.** Emailing to buy a necklace I've never held, from a workshop in Yunnan, with no return policy I can read, no reviews, no Stripe — that's a "save for later, maybe ask my friend who lived in Shanghai" decision, not a Saturday-night impulse. If there was a Stripe button and a clear 30-day return I'd be at 35%.

## Would-tell-a-friend probability: **30%**

I'd screenshot the butterfly and send it to my one friend who's *into* craft jewelry with a "isn't this gorgeous??" — but I wouldn't say "you should buy from them," because I don't trust the buying experience yet. Once they have a real checkout and a few press mentions, that number triples.

---

**Bottom line from Emma:** Beautiful object, beautiful photography of the one piece, believable maker. **Unshoppable in its current state for a cold US buyer.** Fix the checkout, show prices above the fold of the PDP, put your Instagram in the footer, and I'm a customer. Right now I'm a bookmark.
