# Emma — Cold Visitor Walkthrough, Round 2

**Who I am (same as last time):** Emma, 28, Brooklyn, content marketing manager. I make $90K, I follow Sophie Bille Brahe and Mejuri on IG. I came back to YÀN Atelier two hours later because the butterfly stuck in my head. Let's see what's different.

---

## NEW 3-second verdict

> "Ok still pretty, still not sure I can actually buy this."

Marginally less Squarespace-portfolio than last time — that pink Klarna stripe being gone genuinely declutters the top. But the headline is **the same** ("Wear it. People will ask where you found it.") and it still doesn't tell me what I'm being sold. Necklace? Earrings? Bracelet? I came from a butterfly post, I want to know if this brand has *one* butterfly or twelve other things too. Confidence it's "real and shoppable": maybe **45%** (vs ~40% last time). Tiny bump. Not a transformation.

**vs prior:** Same vibe, slightly cleaner page, same category confusion.

---

## NEW would-buy-this-week %: **9%** (vs prior 8%)

I'll be honest — basically unchanged. The one thing that actually gates my purchase (Stripe checkout, ability to put my card in at 11pm on a Saturday) is **still not there**. The Reserve-via-email flow is still the only path. Pulling Klarna and the payment icons made the page feel more *editorial*, but it didn't make me feel more able to *buy*. If anything, removing the payment icons very slightly *lowered* my "this is a real store" signal — like, Mejuri shows me Visa/MC/Amex because they actually take Visa/MC/Amex. Now there's no payment chrome at all, which matches the truth (you can't actually pay), so I guess it's honest. But honest-and-unshoppable is still unshoppable.

## NEW would-tell-a-friend %: **32%** (vs prior 30%)

Same screenshot, same one craft-jewelry friend. The page being less commerce-cluttered actually makes the screenshot *prettier* to send — Klarna pink was ugly in a screenshot. So a hair higher on share, not on buy.

---

## 3 things actually better

1. **Klarna stripe gone.** Visually the top of the page breathes now. It looked desperate before — like a Shein affiliate had wandered in. Without it, the hero image carries the weight, which is the right move for a craft brand at this price point.
2. **No payment-icons soup at the bottom.** Same reasoning. Editorial brands I trust (SBB, Alighieri) don't shout Apple Pay logos at me. Removing them makes YÀN look more like the company it wants to be.
3. **Page feels lighter in general.** Fewer competing trust signals fighting each other. The Hu Yan workbench photo and the 800°C spec get more oxygen now.

---

## What's STILL blocking the buy

- **No Stripe.** Still email-to-buy. This is the only thing that actually matters.
- **No IG link in footer.** I'm coming *from* Instagram. Not linking back to your IG from a brand that lives on Instagram is the single most confusing absence on this site.
- **Hero still doesn't say what you sell.** Category-blind hero copy. "Wear it" — wear what?
- **Prices still hidden** until I click into a PDP. I want a price grid like a real store.
- **"© 2026"** still reads future-dated and weirdly half-built. (I get that today *is* 2026 but it still pings as off — maybe because most sites I visit say 2025.)
- **No press, no UGC, no reviews above the fold.** Three testimonial names is not social proof, it's three testimonial names.

---

## The AI badge: my honest take

I had to *look* for it. On the "How to Wear" image on the Iris bracelet PDP — small "AI 辅助" tag in the corner. My genuine first reaction: **"wait, the photo is fake?"** Half a second of suspicion. Then: "ok actually I respect that they told me." Net effect on trust: **slight positive, but fragile.** Here's the nuance — disclosing AI on a *styling shot* (How to Wear) is fine, I assume the brand can't get every wrist angle on a real model yet. Disclosing AI on the *product hero shot* would have killed my buy probability instantly. So: kept it in the right place. Just keep it off the actual product photography or this brand dies.

**Same place as before — slightly cleaner room, same locked door.** I'm a bookmark, not a customer, until Stripe is live.
