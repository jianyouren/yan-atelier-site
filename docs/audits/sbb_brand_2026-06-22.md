# Peer Audit — YÀN Atelier vs Quiet-Luxury Indie Fine Register

**Reviewer:** Brand Director, Sophie Bille Brahe (Copenhagen)
**Subject:** https://yan-atelier-site.pages.dev
**Date:** 2026-06-22
**Lens:** SBB / WWAKE / Pippa Small / Alighieri register. "Edit out everything that doesn't earn its place."

---

## Score: **6.8 / 10**

Above the Mejuri/Stone & Strand line. Below SBB/WWAKE. Sitting roughly where Alighieri sat in 2018, before they tightened. The atelier is real; the chrome occasionally undercuts it.

---

## 3 things they got right

1. **The hero line is buyer-side, not brand-side.** "Wear it. People will ask where you found it." / "戴出去, 别人会问你哪儿淘的。" This is the single best decision on the page. It earns its space — no overline pomp, no Renaissance lineage in the first fold, no "since X" tombstone. It reads like a confident shopkeeper, not a deck.

2. **Stock language is restrained text, not DTC scarcity theatre.** "8 available · 12 made / 还剩 8 件 · 共 12 件" and "本批 12 件中,4 件已售出" — that is the correct register. No countdown timer, no "selling fast", no red urgency pill. The new `pd-stock-badge--in-stock` ("In stock · ships in 48 hours") is the same idea, well-judged.

3. **Material specificity holds the voice.** "Mineral enamel, 5–10 firings", "800°C", "plique-à-jour · no backing", "the maker chose this one from three" — this is material-led writing. Pippa Small register, not Mejuri. The Daisy and Butterfly descriptions especially do the thing SBB descriptions do: name what is in the piece, name one human gesture, stop.

---

## 3 things that break the register

1. **The new Payment Icons strip (Visa / Mastercard / Amex / Apple Pay / PayPal) is a Shopify tell.** Even at 0.7 opacity, six rectangular brand logos in a horizontal row is *the* visual signature of a templated DTC PDP. SBB does not show them. WWAKE does not show them. Pippa Small does not show them. The comment in your own CSS at line 1675 already says it: *"Hermès/Loewe never show payment-method logos."* You enforced that rule in the footer in June 2026, then quietly reintroduced it on PDP in v3.2. Pick a side.

2. **The Series taxonomy ("No. 01 蝶谱 / No. 02 花信 / No. 03 信物 / No. 04 四季 / No. 05 发饰") is still in the data layer.** I know it was deleted from the homepage in R3. But its presence in `PRODUCTS[].series` and in the COPY object means it will leak back via category filters and PDP breadcrumbs. The 18-brand research that killed it ("0/18 use literary-classifier pattern") still applies. Either delete the field or rename to plain form-words (`brooch`, `bracelet`, `pendant`, `hairpin`) and be done.

3. **Two register-breakers in the testimonials.** "戴出去朋友都问在哪买的 / Everyone asks where I found it" — this is the *hero line, paraphrased back at us by a customer.* It reads as fabricated, because no real customer phrases their own sentence as your headline. And "本来想买大牌, 看到这个觉得更有故事 / I was going to buy from a big house. This has more story." is the kind of comparative-flex copy SBB customers never write. Trim both. A real one-liner ("第一次在网上买这种价位的首饰, 收到比图片还好看") is enough.

---

## Cardinal-sin check — Mejuri DTC milky drift

**Verdict: NO.** Cream/warm-ivory background with charcoal type, no pastel pinks, no gradient hero, no rounded-corner buttons, no "Add to Cart" pill. Photography palette holds (jewel-tone enamels against neutral). The atelier register is intact at the *substrate* level.

**Caveat:** the green `--in-stock` badge (`#4c8c4a` dot + green-tinted pill) is the closest the page comes to DTC convention. It's small enough to forgive, but every time you add one of these "Stone & Strand pattern" elements (your own comment), the cumulative drift moves a millimetre toward Mejuri. Watch the count.

---

## The newly-added commerce bits — verdict

| Element | Hold or Break | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Stock badges** (`In stock · ships in 48 hours` / `Made to order · 14–21 days`) | **Hold** | Text + 8px coloured dot is restrained. The green is on the edge but acceptable. Kill the green for `--hush-soft` grey and it becomes SBB-grade. |
| **Klarna installment banner** (`Or 4 interest-free payments of $XX with [Klarna pink logo]`) | **BREAK** | The hot-pink `#FFB3C7` Klarna pill on a cream atelier page is the loudest pixel above the fold of any PDP. It reads as "we needed conversion lift." SBB customers do not finance a $400 piece in four payments. Hide this for Global market too, or render it as plain italic text without the pink logo. |
| **Payment icons strip** (Visa/MC/Amex/Apple Pay/PayPal) | **BREAK** | See above. Shopify-template signal. Remove from PDP; if legally needed, hide in checkout flow. |

The Klarna banner + payment icons strip are the two single biggest threats to your register that landed in this build. They are 90% of the v3.2 drift.

---

## One paragraph to change

**Current (Promise section, EN):**
> "Made by hand. Kept by the bench, for life. From the first holder onward, this piece is the atelier's to look after. However many hands it passes through."

This is close, but "Kept by the bench, for life" is two metaphors stacked (bench + life). SBB would cut one.

**Suggested:**
> "Made by hand. Looked after by the bench — for as long as it exists. First holder or fifth. Same atelier, same promise."

Removes the abstract "kept by the bench, for life" cadence (slightly too liturgical for buyer-side voice) and gives a concrete extension of who's covered. CN version reads cleaner already; tighten EN to match.

---

## Closing note

The brand has the rare thing — a real bench, a real material story, a hero line that doesn't lie. The risk is not taste-level. The risk is **incremental DTC accretion under the banner of "commerce mechanics."** Each new badge, banner, and icon strip is justified individually and is correct individually. Stacked, they will drift the page from Pippa Small toward Stone & Strand within two more sprints.

Rule for the next quarter: **before any new "Stone & Strand pattern" / "Mejuri pattern" element ships, check whether SBB, WWAKE, or Alighieri use it.** If 0/3 do, the answer is no — even if conversion suggests yes. Quiet luxury is defined by what is absent.

— SBB
