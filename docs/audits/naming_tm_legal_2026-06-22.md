## 3. Three strongest candidates (ranked)

### #1 — **Vitrage**
*Defensibility: 8.5/10.* Arbitrary in primary US/UK/CN/JP markets; no Class 14 collisions identified in USPTO or EUIPO direct queries; clean WIPO Global Brand DB. The French descriptiveness exposure is **containable** — file as a figurative mark (logo + word) in EU to defeat § 7(1)(c) CTMR objections, and add a second-position house-mark ("Vitrage by Hu Yan" on packaging) for fallback common-law rights. Madrid Protocol filing achievable in a single $4-6K USD basket. Plique-à-jour story sits naturally on the etymology without claiming it as a TM term.

### #2 — **Object Hu**
*Defensibility: 7/10.* Composite shifts primary significance away from raw surname (*In re Hutchinson Technology* line). Distinctive in EN; uncluttered in CNIPA when filed as a unitary mark. **Real risk** = Anna Hu Haute Joaillerie opposition under § 2(d) likelihood-of-confusion, since both occupy "Hu + jewelry" semantic space. Mitigation: file with a stylization/logo to distance visually; consult counsel re: pre-filing letter of coexistence.

### #3 — **Dianyun**
*Defensibility: 6.5/10.* Clean in US/EU because the place name is unknown to Western consumers (*In re Newbridge Cutlery* test fails). Registrable as composite in CN under Art. 10 carve-out for sub-provincial abbreviations. Weakness: brand legibility is low — Western buyers can't pronounce or place it, blunting the SEO/word-of-mouth flywheel that named YÀN.

## 4. Three weakest candidates (ranked by total risk)

### #10 — **Clearwindow**
*Defensibility: 2/10.* Descriptive on its face; refused under § 2(e)(1); Supplemental Register only; competitors will use "clear window enamel" freely. **Do not file.**

### #9 — **YÀN (solo)**
*Defensibility: 2.5/10.* Diacritic stripped on examination; reduces to "YAN" which is congested across US/EU/CN/CA in Class 14 and surname-refusable. **Effectively unregistrable as a word mark.**

### #8 — **YÀN Yunnan**
*Defensibility: 3/10.* Stacks the YAN congestion problem on top of a § 2(e)(2) geographic refusal. Both prongs hit. CNIPA collective-mark route possible but slow and expensive.

## 6. The one decision the founder must make

**Choose between defensibility (Vitrage) and identity continuity (YÀN Atelier with disclaimers).**

If the brand will live ten years and operate in US/EU/UK simultaneously, file **Vitrage** now — it is the only candidate among the ten that is genuinely registrable on the Principal Register in all six target jurisdictions without a § 2(e)(4)/2(e)(2)/2(d) battle. The 2026-06-04 research already flagged the YÀN TM congestion; nothing in today's audit reverses that conclusion. Every other candidate either invites an opposition (YÀN/Hu Yan/Hu Atelier), starts on the Supplemental Register (Plique House/Clearwindow), or imposes a translation tax on Western customers (Dianyun, YÀN Yunnan).

Cost of rename today: ~¥25K + 1 day of editorial work, with zero SEO loss (site traffic is still pre-launch). Cost of rename in 24 months after a §2(d) opposition from Atelier Yan Inc. or Anna Hu: $40-80K legal + brand-equity write-down. **Rename now.**
