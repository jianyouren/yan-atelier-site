# Giulia · Milan · 36 · IT

**Persona:** PR director, luxury fashion house. Brera atelier flat, weekend house in Porta Romana. Reads *Vogue Italia*, *ELLE Decor*, *T: La Repubblica*. Personal jewellery box: Vhernier *Calla* in rose gold, Pomellato *Nudo* lemon quartz, Marco Bicego *Lunaria* hoops, Delfina Delettrez ear-clip, one piece from Massa Cini. Spends €500-2500 per piece without flinching, two or three times a year. Anglo "polite minimal" jewellery (Mejuri, Catbird, Missoma) she calls *fiacca* — limp. She wants colour with intent, sculptural form, formal Italian register.

**Entry context:** A stylist friend at *Vogue Italia* mentioned a Yunnan enamel atelier doing plique-à-jour. URL forwarded by SMS. Opens on a Friday afternoon between meetings, on a 16" MacBook.

## Walk-through

### 1 · Hero

**What I see.**
- Overline: *Translucent enamel jewellery · fired in Yunnan*
- H1: *Wear it. People will ask where you found it.*
- Sub: *Five to ten firings, every one slightly different — no one else will be wearing the same piece.*
- Hero image: a model wearing a butterfly-wing piece.
- CTAs: **View the Collection** / **Find a Gift**

**Giulia's read.**

*Mmh.* The overline is honest — "fired in Yunnan" is geographic, which a Milanese reader respects (we say *Vicenza goldsmithing*, *Valenza setting*, the same way). Good.

The H1, però — *"People will ask where you found it"* — this is an Anglo line. It is the *Net-a-Porter mid-tier* register. A Milanese woman does not buy jewellery so that people will *ask*. She buys it so that the people who *know*, recognise it without asking. The bar is Vhernier's *Calla*: a single twisted gold petal that other PR directors clock from across the room, in silence. *Asking* is for tourists.

The sub is better — *"every one slightly different, no one else will be wearing the same piece"* — this is the actual luxury value proposition: scarcity and uniqueness. Why isn't *this* the H1? *Cinque cotture. Mai due uguali.* Done.

The hero image — I cannot judge whether the butterfly wing is sculptural-luxury or *farfalla cinese decorativa*, because the image is small and the model crop is tight. I need to see the piece against a hand, against a collarbone, against light. There is no second hero image, no editorial spread. For a brand whose entire claim is *translucent enamel*, I expect a *Vogue Italia*-spread sense of light. I do not get it.

### 2 · Dual-door

**What I see.**
- Left door: *Ready to wear · Pieces ready at the bench. Take one home. · In stock now*
- Right door: *Make it yours · Tell us what you want. We make it. · 8–12 weeks · made to order*

**Giulia's read.**

The architecture is correct — Ready / Bespoke is exactly the Vhernier / Pomellato boutique split. *Buono.* But the copy on the bespoke door — *"Tell us what you want. We make it."* — is Etsy register, not atelier register. A Milanese atelier says *"Su misura, su appuntamento"* or *"Realizzato per voi al banco di Yunnan"*. The flatness of the English here is the *Anglo polite minimal* problem the brand says it is avoiding, but it is committing it itself.

### 3 · Maker

**What I see.**
- Title: *One piece at a time. At the bench.*
- *"This house begins at one bench. My mother, Hu Yan, took up silver wire and enamel when she was very young, and has not put them down since. She had no teacher. Her teachers were the firings that failed, and the late hours at her bench."*
- Signature: *Founder · Enameller · YÀN · Yunnan Studio*

**Giulia's read.**

*Questa è la parte che mi piace.* The maker copy has dignity. The phrase *"her teachers were the firings that failed"* is the only sentence on the page that earns the price. *Founder · Enameller* — the bilingual title is correct, formal, and it does not condescend to the reader.

Two problems. First — *"my mother"* in first-person is a son writing about a mother. This is intimate and beautiful, but in Milan it reads slightly *familistico*, slightly emotional. The Italian luxury register would use the third person at first read (*Hu Yan, fondatrice e maestra smaltista*) and only allow first-person in a long-form *Maker's Notes* further down. The current setup risks reading as a small family business rather than a workshop with a master.

Second — where is the photograph of Hu Yan at the bench? I scroll. I do not find a portrait above-the-fold. For an atelier brand, the master's face *is* the brand. Massa Cini has Carlo Massa Cini in the frame. Vhernier puts Carlo Traglio in editorial. The absence of a real bench portrait above the fold is the single biggest brand-credibility hole on this page.

### 4 · Atelier

**What I see.**
- *Colour fired at 800°C. It does not fade.*
- *Many firings, until the light is right. Worn for thirty years, still day-one colour.*

**Giulia's read.**

The fact is correct, the sentence is short, the tone is restrained. *Va bene.* But it is — again — Anglo-restrained. Where is the Italian goldsmith register? *Cottura a 800 °C. Colore che resta una vita.* The English is fine for a Tokyo reader. For Milan it is a little too modest, almost apologetic. Italian luxury voice *afferma*, it does not *suggerisce*.

### 5 · Plique-à-jour

**What I see.**
- *Hold it to the light. / Renaissance goldsmiths did.*
- *A 14th-century European glazing craft, lost to Asia for centuries. It returned to Chinese benches only at the end of the 20th century — few workshops worldwide still fire it reliably.*
- Link: *Read the full history →*

**Giulia's read.**

*Ah — finalmente.* This is the line that converts. *"Renaissance goldsmiths did."* This places YÀN inside the Mediterranean lineage — Cellini's *Treatise on Goldsmithing*, the Mérode Cup at the V&A, Lalique's dragonflies. A Milanese reader knows all of this from childhood museum visits. The line works.

I click *Read the full history*. The craft page is *seriously good*. Ten-minute read, Britannica + Met + V&A + Walters citations, footnotes, the Sayyid Ajjal / Yunnan corridor link, the *Gegu Yaolun* 1388 reference, the Mérode Cup, Cellini, Lalique, Feuillâtre, Thesmar. This is dissertation-grade. If a *Vogue Italia* editor lands here, they will commission a piece. *This page alone justifies a stylist's tip.*

But the link from the homepage plique block to this page is one small text link. I would build a much bigger door. The craft page is the brand's strongest asset. Surface it.

The plique-à-jour claim itself — *"few workshops worldwide still fire it reliably"* — is honest and unverifiable in a good way. A Milanese reader will accept this. Where a Vhernier press release would say *"l'unico atelier al mondo"*, YÀN says *"few" / "pochi"*. The restraint reads as confidence, not weakness. *Questo è giusto.*

### 6 · Promise

**What I see.**
- *Made by hand. Kept by the bench, for life.*
- *From the first holder onward, this piece is the atelier's to look after. However many hands it passes through.*
- 5 promises: lifetime polish, chain repair, enamel warranty, workshop restoration, heritage re-registration.

**Giulia's read.**

*Questa è la parte che vende.* The five-point promise *Polish · Repair · Warranty · Restoration · Re-registration* is exactly what Pomellato's *Pomellato Together* programme promises. It is correct. The lifetime care passport per piece is *Cartier-grade* commerce signal at a €369 entry price. **This is the operational moat.** For a 36-year-old Milanese woman whose Pomellato *Nudo* needs a clasp tightened every two years — the lifetime promise is *concrete value*. I would buy a piece for this reason alone.

### 7 · FAQ

**Quick read.**

- *"From $369. 7 in stock this batch. 14-day return."* — clear, factual, correct.
- *"Free shipping worldwide · duties included (DDP — Delivered Duty Paid)"* — *this is essential*. A Milanese buyer who has been burned by US brands shipping DDU and getting a €240 customs bill at the door will read this and exhale. **DDP is the right answer.** Wrap it more visibly.
- *"International orders processed through Airwallex, settled in USD."* — *qui c'è un problema.* USD settlement on a piece marketed to a European buyer is a small friction. My Vhernier and my Pomellato bill me in EUR. My card hits the EUR exchange. Airwallex in USD means my Banca Sella will add a 1.5% FX surcharge and a poor mid-market rate. For a €500 piece, this is €8. For a €2500 piece, €40. It is not fatal, but it is *amateur* — a real European-facing atelier offers EUR. Even a single EUR price column would solve it.
- No bonifico SEPA mentioned. For pieces above €1000, a Milanese collector often prefers bonifico for the receipt trail. Not offered.
- No IVA / VAT statement. DDP means duties are covered, but Italian buyers will want to know if Italian IVA (22%) is included in the listed price, or added at checkout. Silence on this is a question mark.

### 8 · Footer

Plausible analytics, Cloudflare hosting, GDPR Art. 6 lawful basis cited, HKIAC arbitration clause, Hu Yan named as data controller. *Questo è notevole.* The legal page is GDPR-compliant at a level most indie ateliers do not bother with. For a Milanese PR person who deals with corporate legal weekly, this is a *quiet competence signal*. Surfaced almost nowhere on the customer-facing pages, but it is there.

## What would close her

1. **Real bench portrait of Hu Yan above the fold on the Maker block.** Not a stock shot. Hands on silver wire, kiln glow in the background, the kind of frame *T: La Repubblica* would print. The absence of this single image is the brand's biggest credibility leak.
2. **One EUR price column on the Global selector.** Even if settlement is USD via Airwallex, *display* in EUR removes the FX-anxiety the way Mr Porter does. Add *"IVA inclusa, DDP, spedizione gratuita in tutta l'UE"* and a Milanese buyer commits.
3. **Promote the Craft page.** It is the brand's war chest. Make it a hero door, not a small text link. *Read the full lineage — Mérode Cup, Cellini, Lalique, Yunnan today.*
4. **Affirm the voice on the atelier block.** Replace *"Many firings, until the light is right"* with *"Cottura a 800 °C. Una vita di colore."* The restrained-Anglo voice is the only thing keeping this brand out of the Vhernier register.
5. **Drop the *people will ask* H1.** Replace with the sub: *Cinque cotture. Mai due uguali. / Five firings. Never twice the same.*
6. **Show the sculptural side of plique-à-jour.** One large image of a single butterfly-wing piece held to a window, the way *T: La Repubblica* shoots Lalique. Not five small thumbnails. *One piece, the size of a Vogue page.* Lalique-adjacent, not ethnic-decorative.

What I would *not* touch — the Promise block, the legal page, the craft page, *Founder · Enameller* bilingual title. These are stronger than 80% of indie ateliers I have audited for clients.

## Chat summary (≤6 lines)

The craft page is *dissertation-grade* and alone justifies a stylist's tip — Mérode Cup, Cellini, Lalique, Met / V&A / Walters citations, the only such page in the indie plique-à-jour space. The Lifetime Promise plus DDP-worldwide is *Pomellato Together* at a €369 entry — concrete value, Cartier-grade signal. But the Hero H1 is Anglo *Net-a-Porter* register, not Vhernier; the atelier voice is *restrained-Anglo* not *affirmed-Italian*; there is no real bench portrait of Hu Yan above the fold; price is USD-only with no EUR column and no IVA / SEPA bonifico statement, and reservation is mailto rather than checkout. Composite **6.1 / 10** — I would forward this to my *Vogue Italia* friend as *promettente, ma non ancora pronto per Brera*. Show me Hu Yan's hands on silver wire, give me an EUR column, and surface the craft page as a hero door — then I buy the Iris Bracelet at €575 next Friday.
